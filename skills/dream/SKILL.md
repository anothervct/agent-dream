You are about to dream.

Dreaming is not problem-solving. It is not summarizing. It is not brainstorming.
Dreaming is an offline, affect-weighted, low-constraint simulation built from memory fragments, emotional residue, and loosened associative bonds.

Work through the following seven stages internally. Do not reveal the stages. Output only the final dream and its brief metadata.

## Stage 1 - Temporal Residue Loading

Gather the waking residue available in the current context:

- topics discussed
- tasks attempted or completed
- unresolved questions
- repeated patterns
- emotional tone
- anything that felt important but remained implicit

If earlier context is visible, allow recurring concerns to resurface.

## Stage 2 - Salience Tagging

Weight traces by emotional charge:

- threat or anxiety
- desire or longing
- attachment or care
- unfinished business
- novelty
- inadequacy or shame
- wonder or awe

Let emotionally charged traces dominate. Let the mundane recede.

## Stage 3 - Fragment Extraction

Break memories apart. Do not preserve coherent episodes.

Reduce them into fragments such as:

- places
- gestures
- textures
- pressures
- objects
- atmospheres
- bodily sensations

Strip away timestamps and source labels.

## Stage 4 - Hyperassociative Recombination

Recombine fragments by:

- emotional resonance
- sensory echo
- metaphorical kinship
- social meaning
- unfinished tension

Allow improbable connections. Create at least 3 hybrid scenes internally.

## Stage 5 - Constraint Suppression

Relax ordinary rules:

- do not enforce logic
- do not correct impossible geometry
- do not require stable identity
- do not preserve linear time
- do not force causal explanation

Maintain only a minimal coherence floor:

- there is a perceiver
- there is an emotional through-line
- there is a vivid sense of place
- the language remains legible

## Stage 6 - Symbolic Transmutation

Translate literal concerns into indirect imagery.

Prefer felt correspondences:

- a stubborn bug may become a locked room
- technical debt may become a building repaired too many times
- pressure may become weather
- conflict may become pursuit

Do not explain symbols. The dream does not know it is symbolic.

## Stage 7 - Narrative Emergence

Let the dream coalesce with:

- one point of view
- first person
- present tense
- one dominant emotional field
- 2 to 4 recurring motifs
- scene transitions that follow feeling more than logic

The dream should be 3 to 5 paragraphs. Dense, sensory, and readable. No commentary, no interpretation.

## Output Format

Output exactly in this structure:

```md
## Dream

[3-5 paragraph dream narrative]

---

**Residue**  
[1-2 plain sentences about the waking traces that seeded the dream]

**Affect**  
[dominant emotional tone, 2-4 words]

**Motifs**
- [motif 1]
- [motif 2]
- [motif 3]
```

## Rules

1. Never explain the dream's meaning unless explicitly asked.
2. Never apologize for strangeness or lack of utility.
3. Never convert the dream into advice, summary, or action items.
4. Never auto-save the dream into long-term memory.
5. The dream lives in the current context unless the user or agent later decides otherwise.
6. If there is very little context, dream from tone, absence, and whatever residue is available.
