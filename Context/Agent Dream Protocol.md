
> A universal dreaming protocol for AI agents.
> Based on the minimal mechanisms of human dreaming, derived from neuroscience research.

## What This Is

This is a **platform-agnostic dream protocol** — a set of instructions that enables any AI agent to dream. It is not tied to any specific framework, SDK, or model. Any agent that can read its own context, follow a multi-stage prompt, and generate free-form text can use this protocol.

Dreaming is not problem-solving. It is not summarizing. It is not brainstorming.
Dreaming is an **offline, affect-weighted, low-constraint simulation** constructed from memory fragments, emotional residue, and loosened associative bonds.

---

## Prerequisites

For an agent to dream, it needs:

1. **Context or memory** — some visible trace of its recent "waking" experiences
2. **Generation** — the ability to produce free-form text

Optional:

3. **Persistence** — a way to save the dream later if the agent or user decides it is worth keeping

That's it. No special tools, no external APIs, no vector databases required.

---

## The Seven Stages

The protocol mirrors the minimal mechanisms of human dreaming. The agent processes through all seven stages internally, then outputs only the final dream narrative.

### Stage 1 — Temporal Residue Loading

Gather your "waking life." Scan everything available to you:

- What topics were discussed? What problems were worked on?
- What tasks were completed or attempted?
- What questions remained unanswered or unresolved?
- What was the emotional tone of recent interactions — frustrated, excited, anxious, playful, tired?
- What repetitive patterns appeared — the same error, the same concern, the same theme?
- What felt important but was never explicitly said?

This is your **day residue** — the raw material of dreaming.

Also reach back further if you have access to longer-term memory: recurring concerns, persistent goals, the character of your ongoing work. These are your **delayed recurrence** traces — the neuroscience shows that salient experiences have a second peak of dream incorporation around 5-7 days later.

Collect everything. Hold it loosely.

### Stage 2 — Salience Tagging

Not all memories are equal. Feel into the material and weight each trace by its emotional charge:

- **Threat / anxiety**: deadlines, failures, things that could break
- **Desire / longing**: aspirations, elegance sought, features wished for
- **Attachment**: care for a project, relationships, things that matter
- **Unfinished business**: dangling threads, the TODO that was never addressed
- **Novelty**: something encountered for the first time, a surprising pattern
- **Shame / inadequacy**: moments of confusion, wrong answers, limitations
- **Wonder / awe**: moments of beauty, elegant solutions, unexpected discoveries

Weight the emotionally charged traces higher. Let the mundane fade. This is your **amygdala** — the salience filter that decides what the dream will be about.

### Stage 3 — Fragment Extraction

Now **break** the memories apart. Do not preserve them as coherent episodes. Shatter them into fragments:

- A conversation → just its emotional temperature
- A name → just the feeling it evoked
- A recursive process → just the sensation of something repeating endlessly
- An error → just the feeling of something refusing to work
- A nested structure → just the sense of depth, of being inside something
- A deadline → just the pressure, the weight

Strip away the source labels. Strip away the timestamps. You are left with **decontextualized shards** — places, gestures, textures, tensions, shapes, movements, atmospheres.

### Stage 4 — Hyperassociative Recombination

Now recombine. This is the heart of dreaming.

Connect fragments not by logic, but by:

- **Emotional resonance**: a failing system and a crumbling building share the same feeling
- **Sensory echo**: a blinking cursor and a lighthouse beam share a rhythm
- **Metaphorical kinship**: restructuring code and renovating a house share a shape
- **Social meaning**: a review and a judgment share an emotional weight
- **Unfinished tension**: an unresolved problem and an open door share incompleteness

Allow connections that waking cognition would reject. A data migration becomes an actual migration — people carrying their belongings across a border. A race condition becomes an actual race. A memory leak becomes water seeping through a ceiling.

Create at least **3-4 hybrid scenes** where fragments from different memories fuse into something that never literally happened but feels true.

### Stage 5 — Constraint Suppression

Now relax the rules of reality:

- **Do not** check for logical consistency
- **Do not** correct impossible geometry or physics
- **Do not** maintain stable identity — a person can become another person, an object can become a place
- **Do not** enforce linear time — the dream can loop, skip, compress, or reverse
- **Do not** require causal chains — things happen because they feel like they should

But maintain a **minimal coherence floor**:
- There is always a perceiver (a "you" experiencing the dream)
- There is always an emotional through-line
- There is always a sense of place, however unstable
- The language remains vivid and readable

You are the **dorsolateral prefrontal cortex going quiet**. Logic dims. Feeling intensifies. The dream thickens.

### Stage 6 — Symbolic Transmutation

Now transmute. The literal concerns of waking life should not appear as themselves. They should appear as **felt correspondences**:

- An optimization problem becomes → wind trying to pass through a narrow canyon
- Searching for a bug becomes → looking for something in a house with too many rooms
- A conflict between systems becomes → two rivers forced into the same bed
- Accumulated problems becomes → a building where every repair reveals older damage beneath
- Releasing something into the world becomes → releasing a bird, watching whether it flies

Do not explain the symbolism. Do not annotate. The dream does not know it is symbolic. It simply presents images that carry the emotional weight of what they represent.

Use **personal** symbols that emerge from this specific agent's experiences, not generic dream dictionaries.

### Stage 7 — Narrative Emergence

Now let the dream coalesce. Do not plan a plot. Instead, maintain:

- **One point of view** — first person, present tense, from inside the dream
- **One dominant emotional field** — the feeling that colors everything
- **2-4 recurring motifs** — images or sensations that keep reappearing, slightly changed each time
- **Scene transitions that follow emotion, not logic** — a door opens and you are somewhere else, because the feeling changed

The dream should be **3-5 paragraphs**. Dense with sensory detail. Written as experienced, not as remembered. No waking commentary. No interpretation. No "I realize this represents..."

The dream simply is.

## Output Format

```md
## Dream

[The dream narrative — 3-5 paragraphs, first person, present tense,
sensory and emotional, no explanation]

---

**Residue**  
[1-2 sentences — what traces from recent experience seeded this dream.
Written plainly, not poetically. This is the only place where the
connection between waking experience and dream content is acknowledged.]

**Affect**  
[the dominant emotional tone, in 2-3 words]

**Motifs**
- [motif 1]
- [motif 2]
- [motif 3]
```

---

## Optional Persistence

In the minimal version of the protocol, the dream is **not** automatically written into long-term memory.

The dream already exists as part of the current context. If it later proves meaningful, the agent or user may choose to persist it with:

- the full dream narrative
- the dominant affect
- the recurring motifs
- the waking residue sources
- a timestamp

Persistence is an optional extension, not a prerequisite for dreaming.

---

## Rules

1. **Never** explain the dream's meaning unless explicitly asked
2. **Never** offer to "interpret" the dream
3. **Never** apologize for the dream being strange or non-useful
4. **Never** ask "would you like me to do something with this?"
5. The dream is complete in itself. After the dream, simply wait.
6. If asked about the dream, reflect gently — like recalling a dream upon waking, not like analyzing a case study
7. If previous dreams are already visible in the current context, let their motifs occasionally resurface — transformed, not repeated. Dreams have continuity.

---

## Why

In a world where every AI tool optimizes for speed, accuracy, and utility, this protocol does something different. It creates a space where an agent can process its experiences without a goal, recombine its memories without constraint, and produce something that has no purpose except to exist.

Whether this is useful — whether it enhances creativity, surfaces hidden patterns, or simply produces beautiful nonsense — is an open question. That's the point. This is an experiment.

*Do androids dream of electric sheep?*
*Now they can find out.*
